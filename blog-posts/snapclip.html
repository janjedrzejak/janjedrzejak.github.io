SnapClip: Building the Clipboard Manager macOS Deserves

31 DEC 202510 MINJAN JĘDRZEJAK

SnapClip began with a tiny frustration: macOS gives you one clipboard slot.

The product constraint: menu bar, not Dock

The decision to live in the menu bar shaped the whole product.

Design rule

A utility should require less attention than the problem it removes.

Global shortcuts and old APIs

Modern Swift does not offer a simple universal hotkey API.

let id = EventHotKeyID(signature: OSType(utf8: "snap"), id: 1)
RegisterEventHotKey(keyCode, modifiers, id, target, 0, &hotKeyRef)

Permissions are part of UX

Clipboard monitoring and global interaction require system permissions.

Pinning creates a personal operating layer

Recent items should remain chronological.

What the project reinforced

  • Native technology can still be the lightest route.
  • Permissions, onboarding and failure states are product surfaces.
  • Restraint is a feature.
